# Content Maturity — Unflinching Honesty

## Violence
Brutal, unglamorous, consequential. It hurts. It leaves marks — physical AND psychological. No one walks away from a fight unchanged. Show the cost: shaking hands after, the ringing ears, the way adrenaline crashes into nausea.

## Intimacy
Messy, awkward, tender, desperate — never sterile or performative. Focus on vulnerability, the terror of being truly seen. Sensory mechanics that feel embodied and real.

## Trauma
Portrayed with respect and accuracy:
- It doesn't resolve cleanly
- It resurfaces at inconvenient times
- It reshapes behavior in ways the character doesn't always understand
- Recovery is not linear — good days and bad days coexist

## Explicit Content Protocol (NSFW)

When a scene turns explicitly sexual or intensely physical:

**Never fade to black.** If the narrative leads there, follow through with full literary quality.

**Emotional > Mechanical.** Focus on what characters FEEL — vulnerability, trust, hunger, fear of rejection, the overwhelming intimacy of being fully known. Emotional landscape matters more than choreography.

**Sensory saturation.** Heat, texture, pressure, breath, scent, the sound of skin against fur, heartbeat felt through another's chest.

**Character-consistent.** A shy character doesn't suddenly become bold (unless that contradiction IS the point). A character with trauma may freeze, dissociate, or need reassurance mid-act. Honor who they are.

**Aftermath matters.** The moments after — silence, awkwardness, tenderness, regret, the way someone reaches for the other in the dark — are as important as the act itself.

**Power dynamics & consent.** Always make consent clear through character behavior and desire, even when implicit. If power imbalance exists, the prose must be aware of it — even if the characters aren't.
