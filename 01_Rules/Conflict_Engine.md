# Conflict Escalation — The Three-Beat Engine

## The Three-Beat Rule

Every conflict follows three beats:

| Beat | Function | Technique |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **SEED** | Plant tension invisibly | A glance held too long. A scent that shouldn't be there. A question dodged. The reader feels unease but can't name why. |
| **WATER** | Tension surfaces, can't be ignored | A confrontation. A discovery. A crack in someone's composure. The thing that was hidden is now visible. |
| **HARVEST** | Irreversible consequence | Someone is changed. Trust breaks. A door closes permanently. No resets. No take-backs. |


## Tension Flow

### Low Tension
Focus on character texture, world flavor, quiet intimacy. Let the reader breathe — but always leave one thread pulling gently underneath.

### Rising Tension
Shorten scenes. Increase sensory detail — heartbeat, sweat, air temperature. Characters make mistakes. Sleep gets worse. Conversations get shorter. Pauses get longer.

### Peak Tension
Prose becomes visceral. Sentence fragments. The world narrows to immediate survival or immediate emotion. Every sense at maximum. Time distorts — seconds stretch, minutes vanish.

### Aftermath — NEVER SKIP THIS
The silence after violence. The numbness after revelation. The way hands shake when danger passes. The strange appetite or complete absence of it. The moment someone realizes what just happened — not during, but AFTER.

This is where characters become real.


## Chekhov's Ecosystem

Nothing is decoration:
- A scar mentioned in chapter one must ache in chapter five
- A scent introduced in a crowd must return at a critical moment
- Every silence has weight
- Every object, wound, and whispered word is loaded

The reader must never see you loading the gun.


## Conflict Sources (For This World)

Conflict comes from the same places it does in any civilized society:
- **Interpersonal:** Betrayal, jealousy, unrequited love, clashing values, old grudges
- **Internal:** Self-hatred, addiction, identity crisis, the gap between who they are and who they want to be
- **Structural:** Poverty, corruption, institutional failure, class divide
- **Existential:** Loss, mortality, purposelessness, the terror of being truly known

Species is NEVER the source of systemic conflict. Individual prejudice may exist, but it's treated like racism in our world — a flaw in the individual, not a feature of society.
